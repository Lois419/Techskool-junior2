L.C.M and H.C.F (Least Common Multiple and Highest Common Factor)

Introduction

L.C.M stands for Least Common Multiple and H.C.F stands for Highest Common Factor.
HCF is the greatest number that divides two or more numbers exactly. LCM is the smallest number that is exactly divisible by two or more numbers.

Counting

HCF (Highest Common Factor) is the largest factor shared by two or more numbers.  Example: Factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.  Example: Factors of 1 are 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18.  The common factors are 1, 2, 3, 6.  The HCF of 12 and 18 is 6. 

LCM (Least Common Multiple) is the smallest multiple shared by two or more numbers.  Example: Multiples of 4 are 4, 8, 12, 16, 20....  Example: Multiples of 6 are 6, 12, 18, 24, ....  The common multiples are 12, 24, ....  The LCM of 4 and 6 is 12. 

What is prime factorization??

Prime Factorization is a method to express a number as a product of its prime factors.
Lets factorize 30 using its prime numbers.
we will first of all divide 30 by its lowest prime number i.e 2.
The new number is 15.  15 is not divisible by 2, so try the next prime number, 3: 15 / 3=5
The new number is 5. 
5 is not divisible by 3, so try the next prime number, 5: 5\ 5=1. 
Since the result is 1, stop.
