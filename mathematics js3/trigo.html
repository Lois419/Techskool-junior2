Trigonometry

Trigonometry better deals with relationships between angles and sides of triangles. Our Focus is on right-angled triangles.

A right-angled triangle has one angle equal to 90 degrees.  The longest side is the hypotenuse, opposite the right angle.  The side opposite a given acute angle is the "opposite" side.  The side next to a given acute angle (not the hypotenuse) is the "adjacent" side.

Trigonometric ratio

* @ represents an angle

SOH CAH TOA is a mnemonic for easy memory

  • sin @ = opposite/ hypotenuse
  • cos@ = adjacent / hypotenuse
  • tan @ = opposite/ adjacent

    • How to solve trigonemetric ratio

  • Identify the knowns and unknowns.
  • If you know the opposite and hypotenuse, use sine.
    If you know the adjacent and hypotenuse, use cosine.
    If you know the opposite and adjacent, use tangent.
  • Substitute the known values into the chosen trigonometric ratio formula. For example, if finding the opposite side using sine sin @ = opposite / hypotenuse

  • Lets Take An Example..

    Lets imagine we have a right angled triangle with an angle of 30 degree with the length of the hypotenuse of 20cm Find the length of the opposite side

  • Opposite = 20cm * sin 30 (0.5)
  • Opposite = 10
