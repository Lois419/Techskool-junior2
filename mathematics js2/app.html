Basic Operators( ADDITION AND SUBTRACTION)

What is approximation?

Approximation involves rounding off numbers.
Rounding off means either rounding up or rounding down.
The goal is to find a simpler, close value to the original number.

Digits 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 are rounded down.  Digits 5, 6, 7, 8, 9are rounded up. 

How to approximate.

Determine the specific place value (e.g., nearest 10,100, one decimal place, two significant figures) to which you need to approximate the number. Examine the digit immediately to the right of the target place value.

General rules for approximation

If the next digit is 5 or greater 5, 6, 7, 8, 9 round up: increase the digit in the target place value by 1 and change all subsequent digits to 0 (or remove them if they are after a decimal point). If the next digit is less than 5. 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 round down: keep the digit in the target place value as it is and change all subsequent digits to 0 (or remove them if they are after a decimal point).

Lets Take An Example..

Approximate 54.66 to the nearest whole number

  • First of all, check the value to the right i.e 6
  • It is higher than 5, so we add one.
  • Our answer is 55!!
