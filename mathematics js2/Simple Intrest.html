Simple Interest

Introduction

Simple interest is a method of calculating interest on a loan or investment where the interest is based only on the original principal amount, and not on any accumulated interest

Simple interest is calculated only on the initial principal amount. It does not consider any accumulated interest from previous periods.

How we solve simple Interest

The formula for simple interest is (P*R*T)/100.
I represents the simple interest.
P represents the principal amount.
R represents the annual interest rate (as a percentage).
T represents the time duration in years.Â 

Let's say you have the following data set: 2, 3, 4, 2, 5, 2, 6.
The number 2 appears 3 times, The number 3 appears 1 time, The number 4 appears 1 time, The number 5 appears 1 time, and The number 6 appears 1 time.
In this example, the mode is 2 because it appears more often than any other number in the data set.

Lets Take An Example..

Find the simple interest on N60, 000 for 5 years at 9% per annum

  • Solution
  • Applying I=PRT P= N60,000 ; R=9% ;T=5 years ..
    • I = (60,000 x 9 x 5)/ 100 = N 2,700

    Note: Amount = Original Principal + Interest
